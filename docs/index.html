The Debug-Gym Team

Microsoft Research Montréal

We aim to advance research on Large Language Models (LLMs), with a particular focus on agentic coding LLMs. Some of the research interests that drive our work include:

  1. Reasoning — decomposing hard problems into smaller steps, efficiently searching for solutions, and managing memory.
  2. Interaction — reinforcement learning and training models that can interact with textual environments and learn from feedback.
  3. Modularity — building plug-and-play language architectures for efficient customization and deployment.

Concretely, we build interactive SWE environments and synthetic tasks that facilitate designing, training, and evaluating agentic coding AI systems. We distil teacher LLMs' reasoning and coding capabilities into smaller, more efficient student models that can be deployed in real-world settings. We also explore new learning paradigms that leverage interaction and feedback to improve LLM-based coding agents.
